Aloo Paratha

A beloved North Indian breakfast staple featuring whole wheat flatbread stuffed with a spiced potato filling, pan-fried to golden perfection and served with butter, yogurt, or pickle.

Prep Time: 30 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 4 (8 parathas)
Difficulty: Medium
North Indian Vegetarian Breakfast Punjabi

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Warm water, as needed (approximately 3/4 cup)

For the Potato Filling:

  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled and mashed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried mango powder (amchur) - optional
  • Salt to taste

For Cooking:

  • Ghee or oil for cooking (approximately 4-6 tablespoons)
  • Extra flour for dusting

For Serving:

  • Butter (for topping)
  • Plain yogurt (dahi)
  • Pickle (achaar)

Instructions

Prepare the Dough:

  1. 1

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt.

  2. 2

    Add the oil or ghee and mix well with your fingertips until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.

  3. 3

    Gradually add warm water and knead to form a soft, smooth dough. The dough should be slightly softer than regular roti dough.

  4. 4

    Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 20-30 minutes.

Prepare the Potato Filling:

  1. 5

    In a large bowl, add the mashed potatoes. Make sure there are no lumps.

  2. 6

    Add the finely chopped onions, green chilies, fresh coriander leaves, cumin seeds, red chili powder, garam masala, dried mango powder (if using), and salt.

  3. 7

    Mix everything well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Taste and adjust seasonings if needed.

  4. 8

    Divide the potato mixture into 8 equal portions and shape them into balls.

Assemble and Cook the Parathas:

  1. 9

    Divide the dough into 8 equal portions and shape them into balls.

  2. 10

    Take one dough ball, flatten it slightly with your palm, and dust it with flour.

  3. 11

    Using a rolling pin, roll it out into a circle about 5-6 inches in diameter.

  4. 12

    Place one portion of the potato filling in the center of the rolled dough.

  5. 13

    Bring the edges of the dough to the center, covering the filling completely. Pinch the edges to seal.

  6. 14

    Flatten the stuffed ball gently with your palm. Dust it with flour.

  7. 15

    Carefully roll it out into a circle about 7-8 inches in diameter, applying gentle, even pressure. If the filling starts to come out, seal it and continue rolling.

  8. 16

    Heat a tawa (flat griddle) or a non-stick pan over medium heat.

  9. 17

    Place the rolled paratha on the hot tawa and cook for about 1-2 minutes until small bubbles start to appear on the surface.

  10. 18

    Flip the paratha and apply about 1/2 teaspoon of ghee or oil on the partially cooked side.

  11. 19

    Cook for another 1-2 minutes, then flip again and apply ghee or oil on the other side.

  12. 20

    Press the edges of the paratha gently with a spatula to ensure even cooking. Flip a couple more times until both sides are golden brown with crispy spots.

  13. 21

    Transfer the cooked paratha to a serving plate.

  14. 22

    Repeat the process with the remaining dough and potato filling.

Serving:

  1. 23

    Serve hot aloo parathas with a dollop of butter on top, accompanied by plain yogurt and pickle on the side.

Notes & Tips

Potato Preparation

For the best texture, boil the potatoes until they are fully cooked but not waterlogged. After boiling, allow them to cool slightly before peeling and mashing. Avoid using a food processor as it can make the potatoes gummy - a simple potato masher or fork works best.

Dough Consistency

The dough for aloo paratha should be slightly softer than regular roti dough. This makes it easier to roll and helps achieve a softer texture. If the dough feels too sticky, add a little more flour; if it's too dry, add a splash of water.

Stuffing Technique

When stuffing the paratha, make sure to seal the edges well to prevent the filling from leaking during rolling. If some filling does escape, simply dust with flour and continue rolling. For beginners, start with a smaller amount of filling until you get comfortable with the technique.

Cooking Temperature

Maintain medium heat throughout the cooking process. If the heat is too high, the paratha will brown quickly without cooking through; if too low, it will become dry and hard. The perfect aloo paratha should be golden brown with crispy spots on the outside and soft on the inside.

Variations

You can customize the potato filling according to your taste preferences. Some popular additions include grated cheese, paneer, peas, or grated cauliflower. For a healthier version, you can use whole wheat flour mixed with millet flour or reduce the amount of ghee used for cooking.

Make Ahead

Both the dough and the potato filling can be prepared in advance and refrigerated separately for up to 24 hours. Bring them to room temperature before assembling. Cooked parathas can be stacked with butter paper or foil between them and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 1-2 days. Reheat on a tawa or in a microwave before serving.
