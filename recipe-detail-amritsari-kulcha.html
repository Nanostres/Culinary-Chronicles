Amritsari Kulcha

A famous North Indian leavened bread stuffed with a spiced potato or paneer filling, traditionally baked in a tandoor and served with chole (chickpea curry) and butter.

Prep Time: 2 hours (including resting)
Cook Time: 15 mins
Servings: 6 kulchas
Difficulty: Medium
North Indian Vegetarian Breakfast Punjabi

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee, plus more for brushing
  • 1 cup warm water (approximately)

For the Potato Filling:

  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled and mashed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried mango powder (amchur)
  • Salt to taste

For Topping:

  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped

For Serving:

  • Butter for serving
  • Chole (chickpea curry)
  • Sliced onions
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions

Prepare the Dough:

  1. 1

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.

  2. 2

    Add the yogurt and oil to the flour mixture. Mix well with your fingertips until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.

  3. 3

    Gradually add warm water and knead to form a soft, smooth dough. The dough should be slightly softer than regular roti dough.

  4. 4

    Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 1-2 hours. This resting period is crucial for the dough to ferment slightly, which gives kulcha its characteristic texture.

Prepare the Potato Filling:

  1. 5

    In a mixing bowl, add the mashed potatoes. Make sure there are no lumps.

  2. 6

    Add the chopped onions, green chilies, fresh coriander leaves, cumin seeds, garam masala, red chili powder, dried mango powder, and salt to the mashed potatoes.

  3. 7

    Mix everything well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Taste and adjust seasonings if needed.

  4. 8

    Divide the potato mixture into 6 equal portions and shape them into balls.

Assemble and Cook the Kulchas:

  1. 9

    After the dough has rested, divide it into 6 equal portions and shape them into balls.

  2. 10

    Take one dough ball, flatten it slightly with your palm, and dust it with flour.

  3. 11

    Using a rolling pin, roll it out into a circle about 5-6 inches in diameter.

  4. 12

    Place one portion of the potato filling in the center of the rolled dough.

  5. 13

    Bring the edges of the dough to the center, covering the filling completely. Pinch the edges to seal.

  6. 14

    Flatten the stuffed ball gently with your palm. Dust it with flour.

  7. 15

    Carefully roll it out into an oval or round shape about 7-8 inches in diameter, applying gentle, even pressure.

  8. 16

    Brush the top with water and sprinkle some nigella seeds and chopped coriander leaves. Press them gently into the dough.

  9. 17

    Heat a tawa (flat griddle) or a non-stick pan over medium-high heat.

  10. 18

    Place the kulcha on the hot tawa with the seeded side facing up.

  11. 19

    Cook for about 1-2 minutes until small bubbles start to appear on the surface.

  12. 20

    Flip the kulcha and apply some butter on the cooked side.

  13. 21

    Cook for another 1-2 minutes until both sides are golden brown with some charred spots.

  14. 22

    For a tandoor-like effect, you can flip the kulcha and place it directly on an open flame for a few seconds to get charred spots (be careful and use tongs).

  15. 23

    Remove from heat and brush with more butter.

  16. 24

    Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.

Serving:

  1. 25

    Serve hot Amritsari Kulchas with chole (chickpea curry), a dollop of butter, sliced onions, and lemon wedges.

Notes & Tips

Dough Fermentation

The key to authentic Amritsari Kulcha is the slight fermentation of the dough. Don't skip the resting time of 1-2 hours. For even better results, you can prepare the dough the night before and let it ferment in the refrigerator overnight.

Filling Variations

While potato filling is traditional, you can also try other fillings like paneer (Indian cottage cheese), a mixture of grated cauliflower and potatoes, or even minced meat for non-vegetarians. Each filling should be well-seasoned with spices to complement the bread.

Cooking Methods

Traditionally, kulchas are cooked in a tandoor (clay oven), but a tawa (flat griddle) or non-stick pan works well for home cooking. For the authentic charred spots, you can briefly place the cooked kulcha directly on an open flame using tongs, or use a kitchen torch if available.

Serving Suggestions

Amritsari Kulcha is traditionally served with chole (spiced chickpea curry), but it also pairs well with other curries, dal, or even just butter and pickle. In Amritsar, it's often served with a special tamarind chutney and onions.

Storage Tips

Kulchas are best enjoyed fresh, but if you need to store them, place them in an airtight container with paper towels between each kulcha to absorb moisture. They can be refrigerated for 1-2 days. Reheat on a tawa or in a microwave wrapped in a damp paper towel to prevent drying out.
