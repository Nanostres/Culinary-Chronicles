Chole Bhature

A popular North Indian dish featuring spicy chickpea curry (chole) served with deep-fried, fluffy bread (bhature) - a perfect combination of flavors and textures.

Prep Time: 8 hours (including soaking)
Cook Time: 45 mins
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Medium
North Indian Vegetarian Breakfast Punjabi

Ingredients

For Chole (Chickpea Curry):

  • 2 cups chickpeas (kabuli chana), soaked overnight
  • 1 tea bag (for color)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon chole masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon dried mango powder (amchur)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges (for serving)

For Bhature:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon oil, plus more for deep frying
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Warm water as needed

Instructions

For Chole:

  1. 1

    Rinse and soak the chickpeas overnight (at least 8 hours) in plenty of water with a tea bag (this gives the chickpeas a darker color).

  2. 2

    Drain the soaked chickpeas and discard the tea bag. In a pressure cooker, add the chickpeas with 3 cups of fresh water and a pinch of salt. Cook for about 15-20 minutes (or 5-6 whistles) until they are soft but not mushy.

  3. 3

    In a large pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown, about 5-6 minutes.

  4. 4

    Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for another 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

  5. 5

    Add the tomato puree and cook for 5-6 minutes until the oil starts to separate from the masala.

  6. 6

    Add all the dry spices: chole masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.

  7. 7

    Add the cooked chickpeas along with about 1 cup of the cooking liquid. Mix well and bring to a boil.

  8. 8

    Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The gravy should thicken slightly.

  9. 9

    Add garam masala and dried mango powder. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.

  10. 10

    Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.

For Bhature:

  1. 1

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and baking soda.

  2. 2

    Add the yogurt and oil. Mix well.

  3. 3

    Gradually add warm water as needed and knead to form a soft, smooth dough. The dough should be softer than regular roti dough.

  4. 4

    Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 2-3 hours. This fermentation is crucial for soft bhature.

  5. 5

    After resting, divide the dough into 8 equal portions and shape them into balls.

  6. 6

    Roll each ball into an oval or round shape, about 6-7 inches in diameter.

  7. 7

    Heat oil for deep frying in a large, deep pan. The oil should be hot but not smoking.

  8. 8

    Gently slide a rolled bhatura into the hot oil. Press lightly with a slotted spoon to help it puff up.

  9. 9

    Fry until golden brown on both sides, flipping once.

  10. 10

    Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

  11. 11

    Repeat with the remaining dough balls.

To Serve:

Serve hot chole with freshly fried bhature, along with sliced onions, lemon wedges, and green chilies on the side.

Notes & Tips

Soaking Chickpeas

For the best texture and flavor, soak the chickpeas overnight. The tea bag helps give the chickpeas a darker color, which is traditional for Punjabi-style chole.

Bhature Dough

The key to fluffy bhature is a soft dough and proper fermentation. Don't rush the resting time - at least 2-3 hours is needed for the dough to ferment properly.

Frying Tips

Maintain the right oil temperature for frying bhature - too hot and they'll brown too quickly without puffing; too cool and they'll absorb too much oil. Test with a small piece of dough - it should rise to the surface immediately.

Serving Suggestions

Chole Bhature is traditionally served with sliced onions, lemon wedges, and green chilies. You can also serve it with a side of pickle or raita for a complete meal.

Make Ahead

The chole (chickpea curry) can be made a day ahead and reheated before serving. In fact, it often tastes better the next day as the flavors develop. However, bhature should be fried fresh just before serving.
