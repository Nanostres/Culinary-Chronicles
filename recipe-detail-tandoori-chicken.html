Tandoori Chicken

A classic North Indian dish featuring chicken marinated in yogurt and aromatic spices, then roasted to perfection for a smoky, flavorful experience.

Prep Time: 4 hours (including marination)
Cook Time: 30 mins
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Medium
North Indian Non-Vegetarian Chicken Grilled

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 8 chicken drumsticks or 4 leg quarters (skin removed)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2-3 slits cut into each piece of chicken

For the First Marinade:

  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Second Marinade:

  • 1 cup thick yogurt (hung curd or Greek yogurt)
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 2 tablespoons Kashmiri red chili powder (for color and mild heat)
  • 1 teaspoon regular red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi), crushed
  • Salt to taste

For Basting:

  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or ghee

For Serving:

  • Lemon wedges
  • Sliced onion rings
  • Fresh mint chutney

Instructions

Preparation:

  1. 1

    Clean the chicken pieces and pat them dry with paper towels. Make 2-3 deep cuts on each piece to help the marinade penetrate.

  2. 2

    Rub the chicken with lemon juice and salt. Set aside for 15 minutes.

First Marinade:

  1. 3

    Mix the ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, and salt in a bowl.

  2. 4

    Apply this mixture to the chicken pieces, making sure to rub it into the cuts. Let it marinate for 30 minutes.

Second Marinade:

  1. 5

    In a large bowl, whisk the yogurt until smooth. Add all the spices and oil, mixing well to form a thick, smooth paste.

  2. 6

    Add the chicken pieces to this marinade, coating each piece thoroughly. Make sure the marinade gets into the cuts.

  3. 7

    Cover and refrigerate for at least 3-4 hours, preferably overnight for the best flavor.

Cooking:

  1. 8

    Remove the marinated chicken from the refrigerator and let it come to room temperature (about 30 minutes).

  2. 9

    Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). If using a tandoor or grill, prepare it according to manufacturer's instructions.

  3. 10

    For oven cooking: Place the chicken pieces on a wire rack set over a baking tray (to catch drippings). Bake for 15 minutes.

  4. 11

    Flip the chicken pieces, baste with melted butter or ghee, and cook for another 10-15 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred on the edges.

  5. 12

    For grill cooking: Grill the chicken over medium-high heat, turning occasionally and basting with butter, until cooked through and charred in spots (about 20-25 minutes total).

  6. 13

    To check doneness, pierce the thickest part of a chicken piece - the juices should run clear, not pink.

Serving:

  1. 14

    Transfer the tandoori chicken to a serving platter. Baste once more with melted butter.

  2. 15

    Garnish with lemon wedges and sliced onion rings. Serve hot with mint chutney on the side.

Notes & Tips

Marination Time

The longer you marinate the chicken, the more flavorful it will be. While 3-4 hours is sufficient, overnight marination (8-12 hours) yields the best results.

Yogurt Selection

Use thick yogurt (Greek yogurt or hung curd) to ensure the marinade sticks to the chicken. Regular yogurt may be too watery and can dilute the flavors.

Achieving the Red Color

The vibrant red color comes from Kashmiri red chili powder, which provides color without excessive heat. If unavailable, substitute with a mix of regular chili powder and a drop of red food coloring.

Cooking Methods

While a traditional clay tandoor gives the most authentic flavor, you can achieve excellent results with a regular oven, grill, or even an air fryer. For the smoky flavor, you can add a piece of hot charcoal in a small metal bowl placed among the cooked chicken pieces, drizzle a few drops of ghee on the charcoal, and cover immediately for 5 minutes.

Serving Suggestions

Tandoori chicken pairs wonderfully with mint chutney, sliced onions, and lemon wedges. It can be served as an appetizer or as a main course with naan bread or rice. For a complete meal, serve with a side of raita (yogurt with cucumber) and a fresh salad.
