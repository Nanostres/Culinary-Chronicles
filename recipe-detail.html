Dal Makhani

A luxurious North Indian dish where whole black lentils and red kidney beans are slow cooked with spices, butter and cream for a rich, creamy texture and deep flavor.

Prep Time: 30 mins
Cook Time: 4-5 hours
Servings: 4-6
Difficulty: Medium
North Indian Vegetarian Lentils Slow Cooked

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal), soaked overnight
  • 1/4 cup red kidney beans (rajma), soaked overnight
  • 4 tablespoons butter (plus more for garnish)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Rinse and soak the black lentils and kidney beans overnight (at least 8 hours) in plenty of water.

  2. 2

    Drain the soaked lentils and beans. In a pressure cooker, add the lentils, beans, and 4 cups of water. Cook for about 20-25 minutes (or 8-10 whistles) until they are soft and completely cooked.

  3. 3

    In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the butter over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

  4. 4

    Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown, about 5-6 minutes.

  5. 5

    Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

  6. 6

    Add the tomato puree, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 5-6 minutes until the oil starts to separate from the masala.

  7. 7

    Add the cooked lentils and beans along with their cooking liquid. Mix well and bring to a boil.

  8. 8

    Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for at least 3-4 hours, stirring occasionally. The longer you cook, the creamier and more flavorful it becomes. Add water if needed to maintain desired consistency.

  9. 9

    After slow cooking, add garam masala, crushed kasuri methi, and cream. Mix well and simmer for another 10-15 minutes.

  10. 10

    Garnish with a dollop of butter and fresh coriander leaves before serving.

Notes & Tips

Traditional Slow Cooking

For the most authentic flavor, Dal Makhani should be slow-cooked for several hours. Traditionally, it was cooked overnight on a low flame, allowing the flavors to develop fully. If you're short on time, a pressure cooker can speed up the process, but try to simmer it for at least an hour after pressure cooking.

Creaminess

The rich, creamy texture comes from both the slow cooking and the addition of butter and cream. For an even creamier texture, you can mash some of the lentils against the side of the pot while cooking.

Serving Suggestions

Dal Makhani is traditionally served with naan, roti, or steamed rice. It pairs wonderfully with a side of raita (yogurt with cucumber) to balance the richness.

Storage

Dal Makhani actually tastes better the next day as the flavors continue to develop. It can be refrigerated for up to 3 days or frozen for up to 3 months.
